Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Muni Educare and the Muni Model

What is Muni Methodology?

A sum of 42 competency based learner centric methods, designed and developed at Muni International School's research and development, the first of it's kind in a school. "It is not a way of teaching and practicing, it is a way when lived, makes life a way of learning." It values the learner's unique pace, level and preferences because these are more learner centric than society oriented.

What is Inter-disciplinary approach of Muni Methodology?

The Centre-Work in Muni Methodology requires a learner to connect each and every subject to other disciplines or subjects for better understanding of concepts and looking at every subject from multiple perspectives, while following the principle that existence is co-existence. To quote Heidi Hayes Jacobs, "Interdisciplinary: a knowledge view and curriculum approach that consciously applies methodology and language from more than one discipline to examine a central theme."

What is the Student Parliament?

Muni International School runs a fully functional Student Parliament. Students are elected as Ministers holding portfolios like Education, Culture, Discipline, Sports, and Environment. They participate in school governance, staff meetings, and parent interactions. This democratic structure is now being extended through Muni Educare.

What is 'Bully to Buddy' methodology?

A restorative practice framework where bullying incidents are addressed through mediation and mentorship. The child exhibiting bullying behavior is guided to understand the impact and is mentored to become a protector of the child who was bullied. This transforms the school culture from punitive to rehabilitative.

The Muni Model in Numbers

Muni methodologies consist of 42 competency-based, learner-centric methods developed at Muni International School's Research & Development Center. This comprehensive framework integrates ancient wisdom with modern educational practices, focusing on holistic development, self-learning, and collaborative growth. Validated by UNESCO, Ashoka Foundation, and the Government of Japan.
