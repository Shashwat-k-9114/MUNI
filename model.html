The Muni Model

42 competency based learner centric methods designed for holistic development

01

Learner-Centric Approach

The system prioritizes the highest potential of each individual to benefit society, focusing on personalized learning and self-growth. Nurturing students' inherent talents while aligning their growth with social well-being.

02

Teacher as Guruji

Teachers, inspired by ancient traditions, serve as guides and mentors, blending traditional values with modern methods to inspire holistic student development as motivators, facilitators, supervisors, and partners.

03

Peer Learning and Samvad

Collaborative learning through peer samvad (dialogue) builds communication and critical thinking skills. Students engage in group discussions to learn from each other, cultivating respect, empathy, and shared knowledge.

04

Group Reciprocal System

The concept of bhagidari (partnership) and group responsibility ensures active participation, teamwork, and accountability. This approach, aligned with anusandhan (research), fosters a collaborative learning environment.

05

Group of Groups for Shastrarth

Ancient systems of shastrarth (debates) are reimagined in a modern setting where multiple groups exchange ideas, analyze, and critique, promoting intellectual rigor and collective problem-solving.

06

Self-Evaluation with "Am I Able"

Self-assessment tools like "Am I Able" empower learners to reflect on their strengths and areas for improvement. This aligns with the Muni Model's focus on self-awareness and accountability in learning.

What is Muni Methodology?

Muni methodologies is a sum of 42 competency based learner centric methods designed and developed at Muni International School's research and development, the first of it's kind in a school. It is not a way of teaching and practicing, it is a way when lived, makes life a way of learning. It understands that teaching never happens, only learning does.
